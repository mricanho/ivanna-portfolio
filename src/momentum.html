MOMENTUM

EDITORIAL DESIGN | UX RESEARCH | BRANDING | UI DESIGN | RESPONSIVE DESIGN

Editorial responsive design that aims to help their users save time and multitask while learning new content.

Brief

In today’s age, people normally get their news, articles, and readings from some sort of technology. Companies now have to know who they are catering to when it comes to creating an online product for users to read / intake.

The challenge: Create a responsive editorial site thought out for the user persona presented.

Responsive Design:Website, tablet & phone.

Empathize

For this project, we were presented with a user personaand were asked to work with the information given about her.We were given a small bio, her motivations and goals; and her current taste in articles and magazines.

The task presented us with the opportunity to work backward and through empathy research understand our persona to create a website that would address her needs.

Meet Elaine

ABAUT

GOALS / MOTIVATION

BEHAVIOUR


She reads during work breaks. She often works at home, when she’s not at university or the library.

To understand Elaine better we created an empathy map that would allow us to imagine her needs and pain points better.

Through the empathy map and the information provided we realized Elaine is a very busy person with almost no time for herself. Most of the time she´s working an researching leaving her with tiny breaks to enjoy herself and read things that aren´t work related. In her goals, our user persona states that achieving a good work-life balance was a key factor to her lifestyle.

The pains we found were:


· Not enough time for herself

· Not being able to take better care of herself

· Poor work-life balance

· Lack of personal hobbies and passions

The gains we found were:

· Achieve a good work-life balance

· Discover topics of interest as a way to find a new passion

· Invest more on self-care and self-love

· Be more empathetic · Be more rational

DESIGN

LOW-FI

To begin our interface design, we each made a low-fi of how we thought the site should look. We then chose the best features and components combining our designs to create the look of our final low-fi.

Our goal was to create a desktop interface that helped Elaine multitask. To achieve this we created different ways to intake the content and we prioritized it through seamless organization.

We addedvideos at the top of every article that would summarize the article because we believe that visual communication is the quickest and strongest form to process new information. This would save her a lot of time and it would allow her to multitask.

Then we added an audio play feature where in case she wanted to understand the content better but couldn't sit down to read she could listen to the whole article.

Then before the article she could find bullet points highlighting the most important aspects of the article.

And finally the full article to be read.

The idea was to organize the content from top to bottom- quickest to slowest.
