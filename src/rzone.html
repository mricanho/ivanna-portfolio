UX RESEARCH | UI DESIGN | WEB PAGE REDESIGN

Redesign of Rzone Fitness´ website to attract new clients and create loyalty among current ones. Optimization and curation of information to make the site less overwhelming while adding new features to improve client´s overall experience.

Summary

Overview

At present time a business wants its brand to be well known, and so an online presence is crucial to increase discovery for potential clients while enhancing experience for existing clients.

Through research and definition, our goal was to solve Rzone fitness´s problem by focusing on their website and overall user experience. The website is broad and consists of unnecessary information making it messy and overwhelming.

THE CLIENT

Rzone Fitness is an only ladies' gym based in Miami. It currently has four studios around the city. We worked directly with Rzone Fitness Doral.

Addressing the problem

How might we enhance the website by simplifying and categorizing the information so that it increases the member´s awareness to use the website and attract all potential women?

Solution & Goals

· Simplify the website to make it more user-friendly
· Retain current member loyalty
· Attract new potential members
· Creat accessibility and usability for current members to have a better experience.

Analysis

Competitor´s analysis

To begin our analysis phase we made a competitive analysis where we compared Rzone to its main competitors. We wanted insight into how was the competition doing things compared to Rzone and how could we upgrade in general.

Our findings were that compared to the competition Rzone´s website had more information than necessary and that it was the only website that did not have an account section for users.

SWOT ANALYSIS

Now that we understood Rzone better, we made a swot analysis to better target the strengths and weaknesses as well as opportunities to move forward in a better direction.

We found out that even though Rzone has great customer service and technology inside the gym, their communication through the website has information that is misleading accidentally confusing customers and potential clients who could decide to leave to a different gym if the information given kept on being overwhelming and confusing. Having a loaded website felt intimidating and not very friendly.

Heuristic Evaluation

Define

User persona

To begin our define stage we focused on creating the user persona. With all the insight we had gathered from surveys, empathy map and analysis we were able to create a persona that most resembled target, potential and current users.

Meet Clarissa Stevens

ACCOUNTANT, 56

ABOUT

Clarissa is an older woman who is not a fan of the gym but would like to start exercising. She feels that an organized group class could motivate her to exercise and she is willing to try. She has ideas of where she could go by searching on Google and walking around her area. As an accountant, Clarissa works from home and would like to find a place that would get her out of her house and feel a sense of community somewhere.

GOALS

· Easy access to the main information about the Gym.
· Quick first time registration process.
· Accesible ways to register for class may that be the app or the web.

FRUSTRATION

· Getting confused due to too much info at the web page.
· Getting lost while navigating the site.
· Not knowing which gym would be better for her.

Due to her busy schedule she would like to have quick access to information about the nearest studios however she feels an all ladies gym would be best; it would make her feel more comfortable.

To better understand her pain points we made a journey map explaining the beginning and end of her experience looking for the perfect studio to exercise.

JOURNEY MAP

1. She begins by wanting to go to a gym to feel healthier and to get out of the house.

2. She soon realises how uncomfortable she would be excersicing in front of men.

3. She finds Rzone she gets excited and decides to navigate through the web.

4. She soon finds out the current page is overwhelming in information she wont have time to look at but decides to trust the gym anyway and decides to schedule her first visit.

5. As she´s looking to register she notices there isn't an account option and that the registration process is made in stages where you have to fill a form, then the studio has to approve it and later on call you to see if you´re still interested.

6. Due to this process that seems exhausting she moves on.

Design

Moscow method

For our design phase we started by creating a Moscow method that would help us state the features that needed to be there, the ones we needed to remove from the current site and the ones we wanted to add.

SITE MAP

Having stated the features that best served the app we were able to create the site map to state a seamless flow for the user to navigate and enhance their searching and registration encounter. Overall through a seamless journey inside the site we are also providing a smooth user experience leaving the user satisfied.

USER FLOW

Now that we understood how the redesign of the website looked like, along with the needs of our potential user, it was clear the flow the user had to go through to reach it´s goal of finding the right information and creating an account.

LOW-FI

Once the flow was defined we created several Low fi´s that were ultimately combined into a better one. We respected Rzone´s branding and tried to keep the site as faithful to what Rzone´s marketing team had created; however the redesign did make the website a lot easier to navigate through.

Mid-fi

The home page is now simpler however it has more quality regarding visual communication. We decided to have a video of the overall in-gym experience to attract and inspire users.

The about page now shows through videos and images the most relevant features the gym provides. It is all in one page and a little explanation is added.

We felt the testimonial page was necessary to keep, however it was optimized and made simpler and nicer looking. Though testimonials users can get inspire and be more inclined to book a free class and become loyal members.

For the studios, we noticed information was misleading so we decided to have one page where users could access to basic information like area, description and an image of the facilities. From here they can book a free class that would take them to the newly added registration page or by clicking on the studio image a page dedicated to that specific studio where users can access schedules, packages and a link to the studio´s instagram.

Once the user clicks on “book free class” it would take them to the log in/ registration page where they can create a new account or access their account if they´re current clients. After registration the new user would have access to an account that provides them with necessary information, automatizing the process of registration to a new gym as well as general contact with the front desk. Through the new interface the goal of enhancing the website by simplifying and categorizing the information was met. Through it, users will satisfy their needs creating loyalty and elevating Rzone´s reputation through seamless user experience.
